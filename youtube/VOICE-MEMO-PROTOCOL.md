# Voice Memo Protocol — Turn Driving Time into Content

You drive 50+ hours a week alone. That's the most valuable idea-generation resource any content creator could have. Most of them would kill for that much quiet thinking time.

This doc explains how to capture driving-time thoughts and turn them into videos without adding a single minute of work at home.

---

## Setup (one-time, 2 min)

On your phone:

### iPhone
- **Voice Memos** app is pre-installed.
- Long-press on the home screen → Widgets → add "Voice Memos" for one-tap recording.
- Optional: Enable iCloud sync (Settings → Voice Memos → Use iCloud).

### Android
- **Recorder** app is pre-installed on Pixel, or use **Samsung Voice Recorder** on Samsung.
- If neither, download **"Voice Recorder" by Splend Apps** (free, no ads).

### Make it easy to launch while driving:
- iPhone: Add to Siri so you can say "Hey Siri, start a voice memo"
- Android: Add to Google Assistant — "Hey Google, take a note"
- Pick whichever you already use naturally.

---

## How to record (while driving safely)

**Safety first:** Pull over if you need to think hard. No video idea is worth a crash.

For quick flashes of inspiration though, voice memos are hands-free-friendly:
1. Say "Hey Siri, start a voice memo" (or Google equivalent)
2. Talk for 30 seconds to 3 minutes.
3. Say "Stop" or press the phone button.

Memos auto-save with a timestamp. That's all.

## What to record

Anything is fair game. Here's what actually turns into good videos:

### Category 1 — Tool ideas
"I was thinking, what if we made a thing that tells people how many hours of their life they've spent driving?"
→ I can build it + script a video.

### Category 2 — Observations
"Guy in the truck next to me was yelling at Google Maps. Got me thinking — how much of our life do we spend fighting navigation apps?"
→ Angle for a Time Dust sequel.

### Category 3 — Personal stories
"My kid asked me yesterday how many Martian years old he is. Blew his mind. Made me think about how kids process numbers differently."
→ Kid-angle video. Huge reach potential.

### Category 4 — "Huh, that's weird"
"Just realized I've been driving this route for 8 years. That's like 600 Mercury years of commuting."
→ Perfect Parallax angle.

### Category 5 — Questions
"What if there's a planet calculator for astronauts — how old would they be on each planet they've visited?"
→ Niche but interesting.

## Weekly cadence

**No pressure.** If you go a week with no ideas, fine. If you have 5 in one day, great.

Realistic pattern:
- 2-4 memos per week = plenty of raw material
- I'll never run out of video topics with that much input
- Even memos that don't become videos shape my writing voice for your channel

---

## One thing that makes this 10x better

**Be specific about WHO the video is for** in the memo.

Weak: "We should do a video about time."
Strong: "Video idea for people in their 40s who feel time is speeding up — can we show them WHY it feels faster as you age?"

The more specific the audience, the better the video. YouTube rewards videos with a clear answer to "who is this for."

## What to skip

Don't voice-memo:
- Day-to-day complaints about traffic (fun for you but not content)
- Anything you'd be embarrassed to have transcribed (you never know)
- Actual trucking work details (keep work and side hustle separate)
